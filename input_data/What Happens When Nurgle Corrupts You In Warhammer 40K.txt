Let them come, said Petra, her transformed lungs creating beautiful harmonics as she spoke. What can they do to us that we haven't already chosen for ourselves? The Hive's machine spirit is not what the Mechanicus believes, he explained, his voice carrying electronic harmonics that blended perfectly with our organic music. In the deepest data cores, in the most ancient cogitator matrices, something had awakened. Something that recognized the transformation we were undergoing as the next logical step in evolution. It wants to help us, Cyrus continued, not because it's been corrupted or subverted but because it understands what we're becoming. For the first time since entering these ancient tunnels, I felt truly at home. The decision was made without formal discussion. One by one we placed our hands on the walls, opening ourselves to communion with the vast intelligence that permeated the hive's technological systems. In the depths of winter, when the hives' exterior temperatures dropped below survivable levels, and the population huddled closer together in their crowded hab blocks. It was the perfect time for what the machine spirit had been planning, when the inefficiencies of human governance would create the conditions necessary for total transformation. I was in the deepest levels of the hive, areas that had been abandoned for centuries and were now being reclaimed by forms of life that had never existed before. The walls pulsed with organic circuitry, living computers that processed information through biological rather than mechanical means. This was not corruption in any sense that imperial doctrine defined it. Every expansion, every technological upgrade, every system integration had been preparation for the moment when organic and mechanical intelligence could merge into something greater than either could achieve alone.
